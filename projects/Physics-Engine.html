Project Description

    The goal of this project was to apply our knowledge of mathematics for video games by creating a mini physics simulation. The project consisted of four increasingly complex stages, progressing from 2D to 3D. It was developed by a team of three, including myself.

My Role & Contributions

    We built the engine as a team of three. My primary focus was implementing the necessary physics and mathematical calculations to drive the simulation, specifically creating classes for Forces, Springs, Rotation Matrices, and 2D/3D Vectors. I also spent a significant amount of time debugging alongside my coding partner. Despite the impressive number of physics-related bugs we encountered, we ultimately delivered a solid project.

  • Collaborated within a team of three to build the custom physics engine from scratch.
  • Focused on implementing the core mathematical classes required for the simulation.
  • Worked extensively with a coding partner to debug complex physics interactions.

Challenges & Learnings

The main challenge was the complexity of using OpenFrameworks. Since the course requirement was to manually re-implement features that already existed within the framework (such as vectors and matrices), adapting our custom code to generate a visual render was a significant hurdle for the team.
The second major challenge was the substantial workload required to deliver a coherent and functional project. If a team member was unavailable, picking up their slack became exhausting, especially as the complexity of the work increased.
