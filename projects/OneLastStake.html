Project Description

One Last Stake is a rogue-lite card game inspired by the classic game of "War." The core mechanic revolves around cheating: players must forge their cards by "subtly" applying stickers to alter their values and win hands.

The goal is to survive as long as possible through a series of battles, culminating in a final round. Between duels, players can visit the shop to buy stickers and upgrade their decks. However, opponents will not play fair—the AI also utilizes cheat mechanics to turn the tide. The game features entirely hand-drawn visuals.

Some team members remain motivated to rebuild the game from scratch using a more suitable engine for 2D development, such as Godot. We are currently in the planning phase of this remake!

My Role & Contributions

Game Programming & Design: Implemented core mechanics and managed the design and balancing of gameplay buffs and debuffs.

Challenges & Learnings

The primary challenge was balancing the game. Creating a card game is complex, especially when combining it with roguelike mechanics. Ensuring the game felt fair required extensive testing and the use of complex Excel spreadsheets to manage the data.

The second major hurdle was integrating everyone's contributions. We split tasks efficiently, but bringing the work of six people together caused numerous integration bugs. Merging the code and assets from a six-person team was... intense, to say the least.
